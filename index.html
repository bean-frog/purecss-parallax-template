Historical Context

  • Racial injustice traces back centuries

  • Black people were treated as property

  • *some* reform, but Jim Crow laws and segration remained strong

  • Civil rights movement, Supreme Court cases helped

Arrest Rates

in 2021:

  • 528 of every 1300 prisoners were Black (Statista)

  • The population had an estimated 13% of Black people (Census.gov)

Private Prisons

  • For-profit companies running a prison

  • Charge the State for each prisoner

  • More inmates = more money

  • Push the state to enact laws that will increase arrests

  • Corners cut heavily in these prisons

Impact on families

  • Rise in police brutality affects families and communities

  • Losing someone is painful, and losing someone like this is worse

  • Children's minds are easily moldable and the memory of this can be traumatizing

  • Entire communities are also affected mentally

Poverty and incarceration

  • Poverty and incarceration are closely tied

  • Post incarceration poverty tends to start a cycle of re-incarceration

  • In 2021, around 66% of prisoners had been incarcerated previously (First Step Alliance)

  • Released prisoners have a harder time getting a job

Call to action

  • Racial injustice is a pervasive issue

  • The impact is significant and far-reaching

  • We need systemic change including the reform of the penal system.
